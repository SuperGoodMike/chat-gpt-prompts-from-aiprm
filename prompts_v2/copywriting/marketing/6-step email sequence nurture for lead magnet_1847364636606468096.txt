This is the structure for the emails

Email 1

Thanks for requesting [name of lead magnet]!

Regards

Email 2 – Problem & Solution
In the second email, identify a problem your customer is facing that is preventing them from getting what they want. Acknowledge and resonate with their pain. Then, introduce your product or service as the solution that is going to resolve that exact pain point.
Subject line: (Problem Question related to [PROMPT]
email 2 Structure
•	P – Problem followed by fact
•	P – Agitate
•	P – Internal customer problem
•	P – Solution to internal and external problem
•	Strong call-to-action
•	PS – Fact/Benefit + CTA

Email #3 – Testimonial 
Subject line: A how subject line 
email 3 Structure 
•	P – Opening to introduce testimonial
•	Get that person to describe their success to other potential customers. When gathering this testimonial, capture the problem your customer was facing prior to buying your product. Then, have them explain how your product resolved their pain points. Finally, ask them to describe the successful results they have experienced.
•	PS

Email #4 — Overcome an Objection 
Why do most people NOT buy your product or service? In this email, you want to speak to that common resistance, and then overcome it with specific benefits the customer will experience. 
A few things to remember: 
	The objection you overcome in this email should be the main point of resistance your customer has around buying your product or service 
	When overcoming the objection, use empathy and authority 
	It’s always a good idea to include a “P.S.” section in this email that adds additional value and further overcomes the objection
Subject line: 
email 4: Structure 
•	P – Open with their problems and end with objection
•	P – One line empathy
•	List – up to 5 benefits
•	P – List three objections
•	P – Overall objection
•	PS – adds additional value and further overcomes the objection
 
 
Email #5 — Paradigm Shift 
This email shows how your product or service is different than the competition. The easiest way to do this is to highlight how your customer’s current way of thinking is costing them something. This email shows that the way you approach your product or service is different and better. We call this a “paradigm shift.”
•	Make sure to establish the problem you solve first. Connect your unique solution to that problem 
•	Pick a paradigm shift that makes you stand out from the crowd 
•	You don’t need to bash your competition to stand out. Just point out how your thinking and approach is better
 
Subject line: Riding an e-bike is “cheating.” Isn’t it?
email 5 Structure 
•	P – Open with benefits. State what people think. End with question
•	P – Shift the paradigm
•	P – What they should think
•	P – Fact about shift
•	P – Benefit and VP
•	PS
 
 
Email 6 – Sales Letter 
Subject line: Breeze through life on a Circuit E-Bike—now on sale!
Now it’s time to close the deal and get an order. It’s smart to close an email campaign with a sales letter. You can also send one-off sales letters to qualifed leads to get them to make a purchase. 
email 6 Structure
1.	Start with the problem: A good sales letter begins with the primary problem your customers are facing. 
2.	Identify the emotional or philosophical struggle: Ramp up the pain point by diving into the emotional struggle your customers are facing as a result of the problem. 
3.	Brief Testimonial or Success Story: Show how your product or service has solved this same problem for other people by using a testimonial or success story. 
4.	Explain your offer: Focus on how this offer helps your potential customer overcome their problem and get to success. 
5.	Direct Call to Action: Ask your customer to buy your product or service. Your call to action in a sales letter should be clear, direct, and focused. 
6.	Illustrate What Success Can Look Like for Your Customer: Paint a picture of what your customers’ life could look like if he/she buys your product or service. 
7.	Repeat your Call to Action: Include another call to action.
8.	Include a P.S. Section: The P.S. of a sales letter can include a limited time offer or a bonus. 
A few things to remember: 
•	Include the direct call to action and include it more than once! 
•	Be clear and specific when illustrating the success your client will experience 
•	Don’t be afraid to sell — It shows confidence in your product’s ability to overcome their problem and helps position you as their Guide
